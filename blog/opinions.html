My First Post

Kaylee Beyene
October 16, 2017

I recently got back from at two day training retreat with my school’s students’ association. It was a great time. There was dancing, bonding, and other things stereotypically associated with college students. The better part of two days was spent with 13 other people and all we had for company was each other. It was really a great experience and I would recommend other students to run to be on their students’ association for opportunities like this. That being said, there was also a lot of training involved for the upcoming school year. We spent most of the first day learning about governance and strategy and what those two terms mean. We learnt the difference between high-level management and operational management and we even had a mock meeting just to practice Robert’s Rules of Order. It was all very informative, but there was quite a lot of information to learn in a short period of time. For many of the other students, the retreat was mostly a refresher( I’m a first year and most of the others are second and third years.) so I relied quite heavily on their guidance when we were doing practice case-studies. Often the final answers that we would give in these practice scenarios were the product of the other students and I just watched as they came up with the ideas and presented them. I didn’t feel confident enough that I knew exactly what the questions were really after in their answers and so I didn’t speak out when I thought the others were taking the answers in the wrong direction. Sometimes I was correct in mistrusting the answers that the others gave and sometimes not but I still never spoke out. That was fine for this training retreat but in the months to come I can’t let myself get into that sort of passive behaviour. I was elected into this position and so I have to speak up when I have opinions on things. A lot of times my opinions are contrary to what’s popular and so I often don’t speak them unless there is significant cause to do so. I don’t want to cause trouble or stir thing up but, generally, I do give thought to the things I say and if someone can provide sufficient reason as to why I am wrong about something, I have no problem conceding to their alternatives. In a Board of 15 Directors it can be hard to keep true to one’s own character, but it is necessary to keep in mind who you are, why you were elected, and what your values are. It can be hard to disagree with the people you were just partying with the night before but just remember that you don’t join a students’ association to party.
